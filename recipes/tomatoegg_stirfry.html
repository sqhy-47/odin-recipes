Chinese Tomato Egg Stir-Fry

Chinese tomato egg stir-fry is something you'll find on many Chinese home dinner tables. It's fast, easy, cheap, and made with very simple, everyday ingredients. Chinese tomato eggs stir-fry is a popular and simple Chinese dish made with scrambled eggs and tomatoes.

Ingredients

  • Ripe tomatoes
  • Large Eggs
  • Green onions
  • Water
  • Seasame oil(for that nutty flavor but omit if allergic)
  • Neutral oil(like avocado oil, sunflower oil, grapeseed oil, peanut oil, vegetable oil or canola oil with a high smoke point. Avoid olive oil that has a low smoke point)

Sauce

  • Ketchup(tangy sweet taste)
  • Cornstarch(thicken sauce)
  • Whtie granulated sugar(balance salty and tangy) Don't skip
  • Ginger
  • Salt
  • Cold water

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, mix Sauce ingredients and set aside.
  2. In a seperate bowl, crack your eggs and add sesame oil. Beat the eggs and seasame oil for 20-30 seconds.
  3. Heat a large nonstick pan over medium heat. Add 1 teaspoon of oil. Once oil is hot, add the beaten egg mixture. Scramble eggs until they take shape but are still glossy and moist looking. Remove cooked eggs from the pan.
  4. Back into the pan, add remaining oil and green onions. Cook for 5 seconds or until fragrant.
  5. Add tomatoes and water. Allow the tomatoes to cook for 2-3 minutes until they have softened, and skin begins to peel away.
  6. Pour sauce over tomatoes. Allow sauce to simmer and thicken slightly, about 1-2 minutes.
  7. Once sauce is slightly thicker, add eggs and gently fold them into the sauce and tomatoes. Remove from heat. Serve and enjoy. Optional: garnish with more green onions.
