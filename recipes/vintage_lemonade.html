Vintage Lemonade

Vintage lemonade is a classic, old-fashioned drink made from freshly squeezed lemons, sugar, and water. It's known for its tart yet sweet flavor and is often served over ice. This traditional beverage is refreshing, simple to make, and brings back memories of summer picnics and homemade treats.

Ingredients

  • 5 lemons
  • 1 + 1/4 cups white sugar
  • 1 + 1/4 quarts of water

Steps

  1. Peel the rinds from the 5 lemons and cut them into 1/2 inch slices. Set the lemons aside.
  2. Place the rinds in a bowl and sprinkle the sugar over them. Let this stand for about one hour, so that the sugar begins to soak up the oils from the lemons
  3. Bring the water to a boil in a covered saucepan and then pour the hot water over the sugared lemon rinds. Allow this mixture to cool for 20 minutes and then remove the rinds.
  4. Squeeze the lemons into another bowl. Pour the juice through a strainer into the sugar mixture. Stir well, pour into pitcher and pop it in the fridge! Serve with ice cubes.
